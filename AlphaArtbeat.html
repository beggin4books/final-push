Medical

We included images of various medical supplies, like face masks, thermometers, hand sanitizer, and swabs in our mosaic, because these supplies became increasingly important as people tried to protect themselves from exposure to the virus. Once it was apparent that the coronavirus was a serious illness, people rushed to stores to stock up on toilet paper, paper towels, tissues, hand sanitizer, masks, and disinfectant. Supermarket shelves were left empty, and stores started to limit the number of items people could buy at one time. Citizens and companies alike worked to donate food, medical supplies, homemade masks, and other essential items.

Economic Issues

The economic tolls of COVID-19 added another level of stress to an already uncertain situation with no clear answers in sight. Planes were grounded, shopping malls and streets were empty, and the stock market plunged like a cannonball. As university students, we face a poor job market after graduation. We hoped to use this project as a way to reflect on our experiences during this time. It is less obvious how we can display this environment through photos, so we included images of empty airports, empty buses, and a closed movie theater.

Polictical Issues

In this uncertain time, there were a variety of responses from governments across the world. Some implemented strict lockdowns quickly, while others delayed. Even within the U.S., restrictions varied from state to state. As hospitals became overwhelmed by the influx of COVID-19 patients, and unemployment claims grew into the tens of millions, flaws in our fundamental institutions and systems were exposed. Though political issues can be difficult to approach and discuss, we knew they played a central role in the spread and effects of the virus. That is why we included two of Donald Trump's tweets in our mosaic, along with images of police officers enforcing lockdowns.

Isolation

This project was created for the International Space Apps challenge, and we were inspired by images of astronauts going into quarantine for a Saturn V launch. We were intrigued by the work NASA has done on the effects of isolation on people, and found that work particularly relevant in the current situation. As the coronavirus swept across the world and lockdowns were put into place, people found themselves at home, away from friends, family, and colleagues. In this new world of social distancing and staying inside, many had to deal with another effect of stay at home orders: isolation. In our mosaic, we wanted to include images which represented this collective isolation, and also how people found amusement and solace during this time. Within the mosaic, you can find people baking, going outside, and drawing.

Environment

With less cars on the road, and fewer planes in the sky, the positive effects on the natural world were a surprising silver lining during this outbreak. Wildlife appeared in strange places. With spring and summer approaching, we experienced more people taking walks outside, going on bike rides, and spending time in nature. For our mosaic, there are pictures of people enjoying the outdoors.
